The key point here is that we don't start training from scratch. We start from a pretrained model and adapt it to the new task. This means we reuse the learned weights of a pretrained model as a starting point for a new, related task.

Question 11
What is the key difference between localization and detection?
As I clearly distinguished in the lecture, localization is finding the exact location of one object in an image using a bounding box, while detection is finding the location of one or more objects. They can be from the same or different classes.
Question 12

As I showed you in the diagrams, the localization/detection output includes Pc (the probability or confidence that an object exists), the bounding box coordinates (bx, by, bw, bh), and the class probabilities (c1, c2, ...). A simple classification network only outputs the class probabilities.

Question 16
In the sliding window approach, how is the final bounding box location determined?
As I explained, we slide the window over the image by small steps and do a classification at each position, giving a score for the prediction of each window. The location(s) with a high score — higher than a certain threshold — is/are the predicted bounding box.
Question 17
What does an Intersection over Union (IoU) score of 0.00 indicate?
Remember the IoU examples I showed you? IoU is calculated as the intersection area divided by the union area. An IoU of 0.00 means there is no intersection at all between the predicted box and the ground truth box — they have no overlap.
Question 18
How is the Intersection over Union (IoU) metric calculated?
As I defined it in the lecture, IoU = The intersection area / The union area. The larger the IoU, the more accurate the predicted bounding box is compared to the ground truth.
Question 19
Why is the sliding window approach considered too slow for real-time video detection?
Remember when I listed the drawbacks? We need to slide the window by small steps, so we have to do the classification process several times. This means we need to go through the network several times, making the response very slow and not suitable to detect objects within video streams.
Question 20
